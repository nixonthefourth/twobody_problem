\hypertarget{md__r_e_a_d_m_e_autotoc_md16}{}\doxysection{\texorpdfstring{Bug Fix Note}{Bug Fix Note}}\label{md__r_e_a_d_m_e_autotoc_md16}
If you move the star away from the origin {\ttfamily (0, 0)}, make sure {\ttfamily update\+\_\+acceleration} in {\ttfamily verlet.\+cpp} uses the {\bfseries{relative displacement}} between the planet and the star — not the planet\textquotesingle{}s absolute position. See the fix:\+


\begin{DoxyCode}{0}
\DoxyCodeLine{\textcolor{comment}{//\ verlet.cpp}}
\DoxyCodeLine{Vec2\ update\_acceleration(\textcolor{keywordtype}{float}\ m,\ \textcolor{keywordtype}{float}\ distance,\ \textcolor{keyword}{const}\ Vec2\&\ planet\_pos,\ \textcolor{keyword}{const}\ Vec2\&\ star\_pos)\ \{}
\DoxyCodeLine{\ \ \ \ \textcolor{keywordflow}{return}\ -\/1.f\ *\ (m\ /\ pow(distance,\ 3))\ *\ (planet\_pos\ -\/\ star\_pos);}
\DoxyCodeLine{\}}

\end{DoxyCode}


Without this, the planet will fall straight into the star whenever the star is not at the origin.

Key simulation parameters are set in {\ttfamily world.\+cpp}:\+


\begin{DoxyTable}{3}{}{}{1}
\SetCell{c,bg=\tableheadbgcolor,font=\bfseries}Parameter  &\SetCell{c,bg=\tableheadbgcolor,font=\bfseries}Default  &\SetCell{c,bg=\tableheadbgcolor,font=\bfseries}Description  \\
Star mass  &{\ttfamily 1000}  &Gravitational source mass  \\
Planet mass  &{\ttfamily 20}  &Orbiting body mass (negligible in force calc)  \\
Planet start position  &{\ttfamily (100, 80)}  &Initial XY coordinates  \\
Timestep {\ttfamily dt}  &{\ttfamily 1}  &Integration step size  \\
Steps  &{\ttfamily 10000}  &Total integration steps  \\
\end{DoxyTable}


Initial orbital velocity is randomised between {\ttfamily 0.\+5} and the escape velocity each run.
