\section{Sumplectic Integrators}
\subsection{Velocity Verlet Algorithm}

The algorithm works by the principles of updated position, acceleration and velocity to change the position of an object both in 2D and 3D space with energy conserved.

\subsection{Formulae}
\subsection{Position Update}
\begin{equation}
\mathbf{\vec{r_{n+1}}} = \vec{r_n} + \vec{v}*dt + \frac{1}{2}*\vec{a_n}*dt^2
\end{equation}

\subsection{Acceleration Update}
\begin{equation}
\mathbf{\vec{a_{n+1}}} = -G * \frac{m}{|\vec{r^3}|} * (\vec{r_{planet}} - \vec{r_{star}})
\end{equation}

\subsection{Velocity Update}
\begin{equation}
\mathbf{\vec{v_{n+1}}} = \vec{v_n} + \frac{(\vec{a_n} + \vec{a_{n+1}})*dt}{2}
\end{equation}

\subsection{How this works}

After following this algorithm over a chosen timestep (which could be in thousands and millions), we will see a consistent update of the position, acceleration and velocity with the energy conserved over a chosen orbit of initially defined velocity.
